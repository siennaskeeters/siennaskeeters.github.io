# Fall 2026 Team and Program Planning Notes

## Overview

The program should become more competitive by making player growth, measurable skill development, baseball IQ, character, and team culture the top priorities. The plan combines stronger fundamentals and pitching depth with clear standards, fair and transparent decisions, meaningful team-building experiences, individual development plans, and better communication with families.

## At a Glance

### Working decisions and priorities

- Growth is the top priority; wins should follow from measurable skill and baseball IQ improvements.
- Build a positive, resilient team culture centered on effort, toughness, leadership, and “win the next play.”
- Use measurable performance data to guide development, playing opportunities, and position priorities.
- Give players more fielding time and build greater pitching depth.
- Develop each player in at least 3–4 primary positions.
- Have players arrive 30 minutes early to warm up and stretch.
- Run bi-weekly skills challenges.
- Keep 11 players for Fall 2026.
- Aim for at least three teams by adding one more program/team, with players able to move between teams as they advance.
- Continue community service; the food pantry was a strong example.
- Collect money for Fall 2026 now to secure early buy-in.

### Decisions still needed

- What are the final rules, consequences, thresholds, and requirements?
- How will performance be measured, and how will those measures determine positions, batting order, playing time, and other opportunities?
- What are the non-negotiable behaviors and the conflict-resolution process?
- How will coaching disagreements be handled, and who has final authority?
- What are the final player, coach, and team goals and success criteria?
- What coaching roles are needed for pitching, catching, infield, outfield, hitting, and other areas?
- How will decisions be communicated to families?
- Should the team play a more competitive Fall 2026 league, add 2–3 Perfect Game tournaments, or do both?
- How will pitchers and catchers be developed and workload limits managed?

### Immediate action items

- Talk to Drew Vest about joining programs and adding another team.
- Decide how and when to collect money for Fall 2026.
- Schedule parent meetings.
- Build the fundraiser plan.
- Identify high school and/or college players who could mentor or coach.
- Identify and invite potential guest coaches; use a trial approach where appropriate.
- Begin scouting recreational games and practices.
- Draft the program rules, measurement system, communication plan, and conflict-resolution process.
- Create a pre-season and off-season development plan template for each player.
- Create a character calendar and bi-weekly skills-challenge schedule.

## 1. Team Culture, Character, and Retention

### Team building

Plan fun experiences together outside baseball to deepen relationships and support retention. Potential activities include:

- Talyard
- Sienna pool party
- Main Event
- Dave & Buster’s
- Other shared experiences selected with the team

### Character development

Build character development into every practice and reinforce it throughout the season.

Focus areas:

- Mental toughness and positivity
- Not giving up when things get hard
- Overcoming adversity
- Responding properly to failure
- Resetting after mistakes
- Breathing and visualization before or during challenging situations
- Standard team mantras, including “win the next play”

Create a character calendar to give the team a consistent focus over time.

### Modeling and recognition

- Coaches should model calm, positive responses to failure.
- Coaches should recognize effort, toughness, leadership, current character traits, and goal achievements.

## 2. Player Development and Baseball Performance

### Development priorities

- Improve measurable baseball skills and baseball IQ.
- Increase fielding time.
- Build pitching depth.
- Develop physical toughness, stamina, and endurance.
- Prepare individual pre-season and off-season development plans.
- Require each player to develop at least 3–4 primary positions.

### Practice structure

- Players arrive 30 minutes early to warm up and stretch.
- Integrate character development into each practice.
- Add rotating drills for:
  - Strength
  - Agility
  - Speed
  - Calisthenics
  - Reaction
  - Explosiveness
- Run bi-weekly skills challenges.

### Health and performance education

Teach players and families about:

- Sleep
- Hydration
- Recovery approaches
- Healthy eating
- Protein
- Performance nutrition

## 3. Standards, Measurement, and Fairness

Create defined rules with clear consequences, thresholds, and requirements. The system should be measurable, understandable to families, and perceived as fair by players.

Potential measures include:

- Fielding error percentage
- Strike percentage
- Pitching limits and workload
- Infield playing time
- Attendance
- Effort
- Behavior
- Baseball IQ and decision-making
- Progress across primary positions

Clarify how measurements affect:

- Position priorities
- Batting order
- Playing time
- Pitching and catching opportunities
- Advancement between teams
- Other special opportunities

Also define:

- How pitchers and catchers will be developed
- Attendance and effort expectations
- Non-negotiable behaviors
- Consequences for behavior or effort issues
- Conflict-resolution procedures
- How coaching disagreements are handled
- Who has final authority
- How decisions are communicated to families

## 4. Goals and Success Criteria

Set measurable goals at three levels:

- One goal for each player
- One goal from the coaches
- Two or three team goals

The program should define success beyond wins, while still aiming to become more competitive. Growth, execution, baseball IQ, character, and team behavior should be part of the success criteria.

### Candidate team goals

- Reduce fielding errors by 50%.
- Develop four pitchers with a strike percentage above 60%.
- Add two pitchers with a strike percentage above 50%.
- Have no more than one game with bad attitudes.
- Reduce passed balls by 25%.

## 5. Coaching and Mentorship

### Coaching structure

Define responsibilities for:

- Pitching
- Catching
- Infield
- Outfield
- Hitting
- Strength, conditioning, and physical development
- Character and team culture

### Additional coaches and mentors

- Bring in high school and/or college players to mentor or coach the boys; the relationship should be mutually beneficial.
- Bring in guest coaches and try them out before determining whether they are a fit.

## 6. Parents, Communication, and Community

- Hold parent meetings to explain goals, standards, measurements, expectations, and development plans.
- Establish a clear process for communicating decisions to families.
- Organize a fundraiser.
- Continue giving back to the community; the food pantry was a successful model.

## 7. Program, Fall, and Roster Planning

- Talk to Drew Vest about joining programs.
- Ideally add one more team, reaching a minimum of three teams.
- Allow players to move between teams as they advance.
- Keep 11 players for Fall 2026.
- Decide between a more competitive Fall 2026 league and supplementing with 2–3 Perfect Game tournaments.
- Start scouting recreational games and practices.
- Collect money for Fall 2026 now and get buy-in as soon as possible.

## Suggested Planning Sequence

1. Confirm the program vision, priorities, and definition of success.
2. Set player, coach, and team goals.
3. Define rules, measurable standards, consequences, and the fairness/communication process.
4. Assign coaching roles and identify mentors or guest coaches.
5. Build player development plans and the practice framework.
6. Decide the Fall 2026 competition and roster approach.
7. Schedule parent communication, fundraising, community service, and team-building activities.
8. Begin tracking progress and reviewing the plan throughout the season.
